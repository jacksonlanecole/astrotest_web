Why schedule a session with me?

1. Perspective Matters

Physics students can often offer a unique perspective on problem solving in many subject areas, but specifically with mathematics, a background in physics can lend itself well to developing physical intuition for mathematical problem solving. In other words, sometimes (most of the time…) physics is really difficult, and in order to solve problems quickly, you are forced to make the math second nature.

It’s like playing hockey! I don’t play hockey, but I assume it’s like hockey? Anyways… if you go to an NHL game, you watch three periods of overly athletic dudes floating across the ice on ice-skates forwards, backwards, sometimes stopping virtually on a dime on ice-skates, all the while they also are shooting a puck at unreasonable velocities on ice-skates and occasionally brawling over the heat of the game… on ice-skates.

They are also on ice-skates.

Okay, that may have been a horrible analogy, but my point is this: some people are really good at ice-skating, but would have a rough time keeping up with the demands of a hockey-game. In order to even compete at the lowest-level, you have to have the utmost control over your skates. In order to do good physics, you have to really know your algebra and you should feel pretty dang good about your calculus.

Physics students know first hand that algebra and calculus are inherently difficult, so we find ways to make it as easy to grasp as humanly possible. Tips, tricks, intuition… whatever you call it, we’ve got the math experience to help you!

2. Quality of Work

I pride myself on attention to detail and quality of work. If you schedule a tutoring session with me for you or your child I plan to provide them with the highest-quality session I know how to provide. I will to take into consideration all information and concerns given to me and will make sure to hit on areas in which I’ve noticed past students struggle.

To see proof that I believe in doing quality work, and that I can actually do do math sometimes, please look at one of my solutions from one of my upper division courses here.

3. Relatability

Honestly, I recall graduating high school and immediately saying to my girlfriend “I CAN’T BELIEVE I never have to take another math class!”

Somehow, I wound up taking on a degree in physics…

In high school, math wasn’t really my thing, and I definitely didn’t think it was all that exciting, so I totally understand where most students are coming from when they're having feelings of:

  • "Uh... when will we ever use this?"
  • "This is basically impossible..."
  • "I HATE EVERYTHING ABOUT THIS CLASS OH MY GOSH"
I've been there, done that. Now, I think math is pretty cool and it certainly is useful for the things I do, so I want to help others at least hate it less!

Sometimes, honestly, I know I’m not going to change someone’s opinion on math or physics. If you hate it, you hate it, but I’ll still try to point out ways that you can apply what you’re learning to the real world.

Most of the time, the fact of the matter is that you just need to know what a discriminant is so you can pass your college algebra course and you’ll never need to know it again… probably? I can get you to knowing all you need to know for your assignments and tests so that in your course you find success!
